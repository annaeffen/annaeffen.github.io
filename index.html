Biography

I'm currently an undergraduate student at Cornell University studying Economics, Statistics, and Computer Science.

I've always been passionate about understanding and shaping the world by creatively applying quantitative methods to data, whether that's studying patterns in natural language or uncovering trends in financial markets.

Within the CS department, I have been studying collaborative language at Cornell Tech's Language, Interaction, and Learning (LIL) Lab under Professor Yoav Artzi. You can find our published work here: Analysis of Language Change in Collaborative Instruction Following.

Within the Statistical Sciences department, I have been quantifying racial and gender biases in the juror selection process in capital cases under Professor Martin T. Wells alongside Professor John H. Blume.
