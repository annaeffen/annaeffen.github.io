Biography

I graduated from Cornell University in 2022 with a B.A. in Statistics and Economics, with a minor in Computer Science.

I've always been passionate about understanding and shaping the world by creatively applying quantitative methods to data, whether that's studying patterns in natural language or uncovering trends in financial markets.

Within the Computer Science department, I have studied collaborative language at Cornell Tech's Language, Interaction, and Learning (LIL) Lab under Professor Yoav Artzi. You can find our published work here: Analysis of Language Change in Collaborative Instruction Following.

Within the Statistical Sciences department, I have been quantifying racial and gender biases in the juror selection process in capital cases under Professor Martin T. Wells alongside Professor John H. Blume. You can find our published work here: Quantifying disparate questioning of Black and White jurors in capital jury selection.
